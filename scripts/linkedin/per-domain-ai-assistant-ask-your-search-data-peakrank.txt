Most SEO tools share one flaw: they show you what happened, then leave the hardest part — what it means and what to do — entirely to you.

So I built the opposite into PeakRank: an assistant that answers from YOUR data, not a blog post it read once.

The questions worth asking it:
→ Why did my traffic change last week?
→ What should I publish next?
→ Which page is closest to ranking and worth refreshing?
→ Where am I losing to a competitor in AI answers?

When whatthewordsays.org jumped from 6,746 to 12,708 monthly visitors in a month, the useful question wasn't "did it go up?" The chart shows that. It was "what drove the step-change, so we do more of it?"
