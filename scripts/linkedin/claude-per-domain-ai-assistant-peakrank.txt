Open almost any SEO dashboard and you get a wall of charts. The data's all there. The answer isn't.

The hardest part of this work was never collecting numbers — it was telling a busy owner what they mean and what to do next.

So I built a per-domain AI assistant into PeakRank, powered by Claude. You just ask it:
→ "Why did my AI visibility drop this month?" → the exact engines and queries where citations were lost
→ "What should I fix first?" → prioritised from YOUR audit, not a generic checklist
→ "How do I compare to my competitor?" → from the citation data you're both measured on

The key word is grounded. It answers from your own rankings, citations and audit data (retrieval-augmented generation under the hood). When it can't back a claim from your numbers, it says so.

An AI that confidently invents why your traffic fell is worse than useless. Grounding is what makes it a tool you can run on.
